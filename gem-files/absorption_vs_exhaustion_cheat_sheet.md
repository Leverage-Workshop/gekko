**Absorption vs. Exhaustion Cheat Sheet (Volume Delta Profile)** 

## **Absorption (Blue Defense Holding Under Fire):** 

- Aggressive traders (e.g., buyers) keep lifting offers. 

- Defenders (e.g., sellers) absorb the pressure without moving price. 

- Delta profile shows a thick cluster of blue bins near one price. 

- Often appears in a grind or slow pullback zone. 

- Tempo is slower: develops over 2-5 bars. 

- Suggests strength if price doesn't break down. 

- Tactical Analogy: Shield wall holds as attackers charge repeatedly. 

## **Exhaustion (Red Collapse After Final Push):** 

- Aggressors (e.g., buyers) surge in force, often at the top of a leg. 

- Little to no follow-through after this final push. 

- Delta profile shows a tall tapering cone of blue bins. 

- Often concentrated in just 1-2 bars. 

- Price may snap back quickly afterward. 

- Suggests a failed continuation and possible reversal. 

- Tactical Analogy: Attack formation breaks down mid-charge and gets flanked. 

## **Comparison Table:** 

Feature          | Absorption             | Exhaustion 

-----------------|------------------------|------------------------ 

Tempo            | Gradual (2-5 bars)     | Sudden (1-2 bars) 

Delta Bins       | Clustered              | Tapered 

Price Behavior   | Stalled                | Blowoff + snapback 

Likely Outcome   | Continuation or trap   | Reversal or fade 

Tape Behavior    | Persistent pressure    | Final surge, then silence 

## **Use in Practice:** 

- Absorption in pullback zone = rebid likely, trend may continue. 

- Exhaustion at key resistance = high trap/reversal probability. 

- Combine with DOM: absorption shows refill, exhaustion shows void.
