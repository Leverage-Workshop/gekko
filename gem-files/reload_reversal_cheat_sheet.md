**Controlled Flush & Reload at Structure (Aggressive Downtrend Reversal)** 

## **Setup Summary:** 

- Fast, aggressive down move - price drops sharply with minimal pullbacks. 

- The drop ends at a key structural level: edge of a multi-day range, volume shelf, or prior value. 

- Sudden reversal follows - often fast and one-sided upward. 

## **Tactical Reframe:** 

Phase 1: Strategic Retreat 

- Big players unload into a fast move, pulling price toward key structure. 

- Retail capitulates while smart money flattens or exits. 

## Phase 2: Structural Anchoring 

- Price hits a clear boundary - often a composite edge or VWAP band. 

- Heavy red delta prints at the low, then blue begins to appear aggressively. 

- DOM may show red sweep followed by blue size reloading lower. 

## Phase 3: Reversal 

- Blue tape takes initiative. 

- Price moves quickly upward and does not revisit the low. 

- Often leads to VWAP reversion or trend reversal. 

## **Clues to Watch:** 

Delta Profile: 

- Large red clusters at lows. 

- Sudden transition to blue delta spike (imbalance flip). 

## DOM: 

- Large sell sweep followed by blue limit orders refilling. 

- Tape shows blue aggression (buyers lifting offers). 

## VWAP/Structure: 

- Outer band hit at edge of composite. 

**Controlled Flush & Reload at Structure (Aggressive Downtrend Reversal)** 

- Price hits multi-day range low or value shelf. 

## Price Action: 

- No continuation lower after initial flush. 

- Clean reversal with no lower high. 

## **Execution Plan:** 

## Entry: 

- Aggressive: on blue aggression + DOM support. 

- Conservative: on reclaim of breakdown level or VWAP. 

## Target: 

- VWAP, prior range midpoint, or opposite edge. 

## Stop: 

- Just below structural low where blue delta first appeared. 

## **Psychological Drivers:** 

- Retail panic fuels the flush. 

- Smart money waits to reload at high R/R location. 

- Momentum chasers are trapped at the bottom. 

- Reversal happens where imbalance corrects the most.
